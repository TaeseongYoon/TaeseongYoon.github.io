About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering at KAIST, advised by Professor Heeyoung Kim in the Industrial Statistics Lab. My research aims to develop uncertainty-aware deep learning methods that remain reliable under distribution shift, class imbalance, noisy labels, and industrial anomaly detection scenarios.

My recent work focuses on flexible evidential learning frameworks, interpretable uncertainty-aware classification, and graph-based probabilistic models for multivariate time-series anomaly detection.
