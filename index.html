About

Researching reliable deep learning under uncertainty.

I am a Ph.D. candidate at KAIST advised by Professor Heeyoung Kim. My research focuses on uncertainty-aware deep learning methods that remain reliable under distribution shift, class imbalance, noisy labels, and industrial anomaly detection scenarios.

My recent work develops flexible evidential learning frameworks and interpretable uncertainty-aware classification models, with applications to robust classification and multivariate time-series anomaly detection.

Selected Work

Publications

My work focuses on principled and practical uncertainty estimation for modern deep learning systems.

NeurIPS 2025

Uncertainty Estimation by Flexible Evidential Deep Learning

Taeseong Yoon and Heeyoung Kim

Flexible Evidential Deep Learning extends evidential deep learning by predicting a flexible Dirichlet distribution over class probabilities, improving uncertainty generalization under classical, long-tailed, and noisy in-distribution settings.

OpenReview arXiv Code
ICML 2024

Uncertainty Estimation by Density Aware Evidential Deep Learning

Taeseong Yoon and Heeyoung Kim

Density Aware Evidential Deep Learning improves evidential uncertainty estimation by incorporating feature-space density information at prediction time and introducing a parameterization designed to improve classification and OOD detection.
